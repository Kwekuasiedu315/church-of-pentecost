About Us

The Church of Pentecost is a global ministry with its headquarters in Accra, Ghana. The church was founded in 1931 by Pastor James McKeown, an Irish missionary who was sent to Ghana by the Apostolic Church, Bradford, England. Today, the Church of Pentecost has more than 3 million members in over 100 countries around the world.

Our mission is to proclaim the gospel of Jesus Christ to all nations, and to make disciples of all people. We believe in the power of the Holy Spirit to transform lives and communities, and we seek to be a light in the world by sharing the love of Christ with others.

Our Beliefs

  • We believe in the Bible as the inspired and authoritative Word of God.
  • We believe in one God, who exists in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.
  • We believe in the death and resurrection of Jesus Christ as the only way to salvation.
  • We believe in the baptism of believers by immersion in water.
  • We believe in the indwelling and empowerment of the Holy Spirit for believers.

Our Mission

At the Church of Pentecost, our mission is to spread the gospel of Jesus Christ and make disciples of all nations. We believe that through faith in Christ, people can find salvation, healing, and wholeness in all areas of life.

Through our various ministries and outreach programs, we seek to share the love of Christ with those in need and provide opportunities for growth and spiritual development.

Our Leadership

The Church of Pentecost is led by a General Council, which is elected every five years. The General Council is responsible for overseeing the work of the church, and for setting its policies and direction. The current Chairman of the General Council is Apostle Eric Nyamekye.
